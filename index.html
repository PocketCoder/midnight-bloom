Writer's Statement

I began writing Midnight Bloom as a distraction. It scratched an itch that kept me going through lockdown, unemployment, and a post-uni crisis, where I was deciding which direction I wanted my life to go.


The initial idea stuck with me because it was a story I hadn’t seen told. I grew up surrounded by a masculinity that was, very present but nevertheless always out of reach for me. I wanted to explore this theme, having characters live their lives partly through the lens of my own experience. I wanted characters that most audiences would stereotype – people that might be written oﬀ and given little careful thought. Then I could expose their feelings and their sides unseen. How would love prevail over restrictive hyper-masculinity, societal expectations, and a whole host of other odds?


Through the writing process I explored my own identity and my experiences growing up. My perspective undoubtedly shaped the script and the characters. I wanted to see if love would endure its exposure to criticism and ridicule in Aaron and Jamie’s world. I wanted to test the connection a father has with his daughter, and demonstrate that there’s room for it alongside other personal bonds. I hoped, and still do, that seeing male tenderness, male love, and male friendship on screen invites conversation, connection, and, dare I say, change.


I currently work at a youth club and see all diﬀerent manifestations of young life, with its struggles, hopes and opportunities. I have the privilege of sharing lives with young people as they grow. I’ve seen softer, more tender sides being lost and gradually regained on complex journeys of discovery. Midnight Bloom exists to show how two halves of ourselves can exist as one. Media representations of these things have moved a long way since I was in school, even since I started university, but I want to push it further. I want to be a part of the conversation. I want to add to the collection of great films that explore life growing up in a poor city, being trapped by circumstance, and having the resolve to power through regardless. The script, for me, is about finding everything good about life, whatever our circumstances, and pushing that to the forefront of our vision.
